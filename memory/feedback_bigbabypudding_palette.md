For all @bigbabypudding (girlfriend's banana pudding brand, Notting Hill) design work — Instagram tiles, posters, cards, packaging, mockups, future brand assets — use these locked rules:

## Two-color palette
**Primary (only colors used as backgrounds):**
- `#faf8f0` cream (UPDATED 2026-05-17 from #fffae2 — old was too yellow/vintage. New is modern off-white, Sweetgreen/Aesop vibe) (default tile/poster background)
- `#39251c` dark brown (typography, character outlines, dark tile backgrounds)

**Restricted (NOT as tile backgrounds):**
- `#ffe256` banana yellow — appears only inside character art (banana hair, peel) and on the actual cup product
- `#c2dff6` baby blue — appears only in real cup-rim photos (rim is striped blue/cream)

**Pastel accents (NEW rule, 2026-05-16) — allowed sparingly to break cream tile monotony, but ONLY where contextually justified:**
- Soft pink `#f5d4d8`
- Soft blue `#d0e0e8`
- Soft peach `#f5d8ba`
- Soft cream-yellow `#f0e6bc`
- Soft lavender `#dcc8e0`

**CONFIRMED IN USE:** Notting Hill terrace houses (one pastel per house).

**TRIED AND REJECTED (2026-05-16):** pink halo behind logo, peach ticket around Deliveroo text, blue spotlight behind peel, peach tablecloth under cups. User found these "added randomly" and felt they broke the minimal vibe. **Do NOT apply pastel halos / shapes behind characters or text blocks as a default.**

**Right approach for future tiles:** Use pastels only when the element is *contextually* coloured (e.g. Notting Hill houses ARE pastel in real life). Do not paint abstract decorative halos/grounds.

**Brown-line accents — also rejected as default.** User explicitly said "lines that added randomly" — don't add baselines or dividers to every cream tile. Brown lines OK only when contextually meaningful (e.g. a sidewalk in Notting Hill, a counter in a process shot).

**Crayon-coloring aesthetic (KEY note for future hand-drawn elements):** When a hand-drawn element IS coloured, the colour should look like it was applied with a crayon — slightly imperfect, sketchy, with small inconsistencies. Avoid clean perfect digital fills. Goal: warmth and simplicity via "kid's coloring book" feel. Approaches: rough/uneven fill edges, slight transparency variation, hand-drawn colour patches that slightly miss the lines, repeated short strokes building up colour. Save this rule for when we actually colour elements.

**Banned (never use):**
- `#d29356` tan (original colour-sheet tone, too saturated)
- Saturated pinks, blues, greens, reds — anything not in the soft pastel range above

## Fonts
The "Big Baby Banana Pudding" logo uses a chunky hand-drawn brush script. To match in new designs:
- **Headlines / display:** Bradley Hand Bold (`/System/Library/Fonts/Supplemental/Bradley Hand Bold.ttf`) — closest match to the logo's brush-script feel.
- **Body / small text:** Chalkboard SE Bold (`/System/Library/Fonts/Supplemental/ChalkboardSE.ttc` index 2) — hand-drawn but readable at small sizes.
- **Never use** Helvetica, Arial, or other clean sans-serifs — they fight the storybook brand voice.

## Why
User wants a simple, cozy, storybook-feeling brand. Two colors + hand-drawn fonts is the discipline that holds it together — beignetbeignet does the same with olive/cream + their script wordmark. Anything more starts to feel scattered.

## How to apply
- Tile backgrounds = cream OR brown only
- All typography = Bradley Hand Bold (headlines) + Chalkboard SE Bold (body), brown on cream / cream on brown
- Highlight covers = cream discs with brown character art
- Profile pic background = cream
- Yellow lift comes from the character art organically; never paint a tile yellow

## Caption voice rule (NEW, 2026-05-17) — for ALL Instagram posts

**Captions must SUGGEST a story, not EXPLAIN one.** User rejected the descriptive style ("Unripe = no flavour. Overripe = darkens the pudding...") as "too informative, normal caption". Wants intrigue + personality + brevity.

**Rules:**
- Max 6 words is the sweet spot. 8 is the upper limit.
- Never list quality bullets. Never explain the brand.
- Give bananas personality. Treat ingredients like characters.
- Slight mystery > full information. Make people wonder.
- One sentence, period at end.
- Examples of the locked voice: *"Three walked in. One got hired." / "Auditions every morning." / "Most don't make the cut." / "Sorry, green ones." / "Goldilocks works here."*
- Words to avoid in captions: indulgent, delicious, yummy, drool, OBSESSED, quality, premium, sourced, hand-picked (the action, not the word — but never the word).

**How to apply:** Every caption I draft for @bigbabypudding posts should hint at the story the visual is telling, not narrate it. If a caption reads like a product description, scrap it.

## Locked illustration direction (2026-05-17) — based on user-approved "Today's batch" cup tile

**The cup tile (3 cream cups on brown bg + small yellow banana) is THE reference for all hand-drawn illustrations going forward.** User said this is "perfect" and wants this direction for the rest.

**Visual language rules:**
- **Bold filled shapes** define elements — not thin outlines floating in space
- Cream-filled silhouettes against brown bg work best for drama
- OR brown line drawings on cream bg for simpler/minimal moments
- Brown lines for rim details, seams, accent marks
- ONE small colour accent per scene (yellow banana, pastel house) — rest is brown + cream
- Clean shapes with slight hand-drawn imperfection — NOT messy scribbles
- Minimal interior detail (no busy textures, no random dots, no decorative blush/sparkles)
- Generous breathing room around subjects

**Two acceptable modes:**
1. **Coloured mode** (like the cup tile): brown bg + cream/yellow filled shapes + small character accent. For: cups, packaging, hero scenes
2. **Outlined mode** (HeyTea-style): cream bg + brown line drawings only, no fill. For: text-heavy tiles, single objects, calendar/clock-type illustrations

**Mix throughout the grid for rhythm** — not every tile needs the same mode.

**For illustration STYLE: @heytea Instagram**
- Simple hand-drawn, "kid drawing" feel
- Loose lines, friendly imperfection
- Confident but not slick
- **Application to Big Baby:** all illustrations should look like a sweet, slightly wonky kids' book — never digital-perfect, never overly polished. Brown line art on cream.

**For COLOURING: kid with crayons + brand palette**
- Look like a child coloured it with pastel crayons OR with the brown brand crayon
- Slightly imperfect fill (small gaps, uneven coverage)
- Pastel palette already locked: soft pink, soft blue, soft peach, soft cream-yellow, soft lavender
- Plus the dark brown for line art
- NO clean digital flat fills — always crayon texture
- See [[feedback-bigbabypudding-palette]] pastel section for the exact hex values

## Locked wafer design (2026-05-17) — for ALL future wafer references

When drawing a Nilla wafer anywhere (illustration tile, anatomy diagram, reel, cup mockup, future tile), use this exact spec:

- **Background:** pastel yellow `#f0e6bc` (also unlocked pastel yellow as a tile bg colour for the grid — first non-cream/non-brown bg permitted)
- **Body:** deeper nilla tan `#b8854d` (NOT the lighter `#d4a574` — too pale on pastel bg)
- **Shape:** clean nearly-round circle, *very* subtle hand-drawn imperfection only (jitter ≤2px on a ~260px radius — scribbly was rejected)
- **Outline:** single thin brown line, 5px, with matching ≤2px jitter
- **No cream halo** around the wafer (was rejected — reads as torn paper / messy)
- **Face:** tiny + tight + simple — two small brown dot eyes (~10-11px radius, ~50px apart on a 260px wafer), small brown smile arc (5px line, 36px wide)
- **NO blush, NO cheeks, NO ridge lines, NO mouth interior**

**Why:** User picked this combination (option "C2") over the brown-bg version because the warm pastel yellow gives a "vanilla" association that fits the wafer, breaks grid monotony, and the deeper nilla solves the warm-on-warm bg blend issue. Hand-drawn but clean (not scribbly) was the explicit lock.

**How to apply:** Reference script: `/Users/amir/Desktop/bigbabypudding/lock_illust_03_wafer.py`. Re-use the `draw_locked_wafer()` function (or copy its parameters) anytime a wafer needs to appear in any other illustration or animation. Don't redraw freehand — the params are locked.

## Inner-frame border (NEW design pattern, 2026-05-16)

User loves the clean look of the Spoon Lift placeholder tile (pos 9): brown background with a thin inner border frame ~30px in from the tile edge, drawn in the contrast colour (cream on brown / brown on cream).

**When to use:** future poster/text-only tiles, announcement tiles, and tiles where minimalism + structure are the goal. Acts like a "frame" or "card" inside the tile and gives instant definition without busy elements.

**How:** draw a 4-6px rectangle outline inset by ~30px from each edge, in the contrast colour. No rounding or rounding ~8px max. Use sparingly — not on every tile, only when the design would otherwise feel empty.

**Worked well on:** Spoon Lift, Cup Stack placeholder. Worth trying for: future "Coming soon" tiles, sale announcements, "Sold out" tiles, weekly highlight covers.
