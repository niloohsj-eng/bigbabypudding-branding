# Lifestyle — Out-and-about and behind-the-scenes

Photos that aren't strictly "product hero." Candid, contextual, atmospheric. The shots that make followers feel like they know the brand.

**Cadence:** **1x per week** — these are flavor, not main course.

## What goes here

- Cup held mid-stride on Portobello, Lancaster Road, any pastel mews
- Cup resting on a bench, window sill, café table
- Hands prepping in the kitchen (cracking eggs, slicing bananas, layering wafers)
- The fridge full of cups ready for tomorrow's deliveries
- A Deliveroo rider with the bag (with permission)
- A customer enjoying it (with permission)
- The space — counter, ingredients, your apron, the chaos of a batch day

## What does NOT go here

- Posed product photos (those go in `photos/`)
- Illustrated graphics (those go in `illustrations/`)

## How to take great lifestyle shots

- Use the iPhone "Photographic Style" set to slightly warm
- Shoot in soft window light, never flash
- Get LOW (hip height or below) and ANGLE the phone slightly for more interesting compositions
- Capture **details** — hands, wafer crumbs on the counter, a folded apron, condensation on a fridge door
- Avoid posed smiles directly at camera. Candid > posed every time.
- Take 3–5 shots per moment so you can pick the best

## Tips for the Notting Hill street shots

- **Best light:** late afternoon golden hour OR soft cloudy days
- **Best blocks:** Lancaster Rd (most-Instagrammed pastels), Portobello Rd, St Luke's Mews, Powis Square
- **Avoid:** red/green facades (clash brand) and direct midday sun (washes everything out)
- **Carry:** the cup in a brown paper bag for that "just picked up" look

## Caption rules

Same as illustrations and photos. For lifestyle, captions can be slightly more atmospheric:

- *Caught in the wild on Portobello.*
- *Last batch out the door.*
- *Sunday morning ritual.*
- *Walking it home.*
