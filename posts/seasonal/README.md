# Seasonal — Holidays, occasions, themed posts

Posts tied to dates: Easter, Halloween, Christmas, Valentine's, Mother's Day, New Year, Notting Hill Carnival, Wimbledon, the first day of spring.

**Cadence:** as occasions arise. Aim for **1 strong seasonal post per major holiday**.

## What goes here

- Themed illustrations (hand-drawn in the same style as `illustrations/` but with a holiday twist)
- Seasonal photos (cup with a Christmas wreath, Easter table, picnic blanket for summer)
- Holiday announcement posters (Christmas opening hours, Valentine's special, etc.)

## Major UK dates worth posting around

| Date | What | Caption tone |
|---|---|---|
| Feb 14 | Valentine's | Soft, sweet, share-with-someone |
| Mar/Apr | Easter | Family + fresh start |
| Mar 31 | Mother's Day UK | Warmth, gratitude |
| May 5 | Bank Holiday | Long weekend = pudding weekend |
| Jun 21 | Summer solstice | Sunny, light, picnic |
| Aug | Notting Hill Carnival (last weekend Aug) | Local pride, "we live here too" |
| Oct 31 | Halloween | Playful, treat-themed |
| Nov 5 | Bonfire Night | Cozy, warm, indoor |
| Dec 24-26 | Christmas | Festive, warm |
| Dec 31 | NYE | Year-end reflection |

## How to make a seasonal illustration

Open `build_illustrations.py`, copy any existing function, swap the focal element. Examples:
- Easter → cup with a hand-drawn Easter egg beside (similar to the banana style)
- Christmas → cup with a tiny Santa hat on the cream
- Valentine's → cup with a hand-drawn heart cloud above

Keep it the same brown-line-on-cream style. Don't introduce festive reds and greens (off-palette). The character's yellow is the only color outside brown/cream.

## What does NOT go here

- General everyday posts (those go in `illustrations/`, `photos/`, `lifestyle/`)
- Promotional sales (those should go through `announcements/` if we ever add one, or just into `photos/`)
